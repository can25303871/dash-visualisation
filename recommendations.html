Recommendations Page

To help pick our in-game team for the next three gameweeks, we can consult Fixture Difficulty Ratings (FDR), which are a way of quantifying, in numerical form, how difficult a match will be for a given team, and consequently how the team can be expected to perform.
The Premier League website explains FDR as follows:

FDR Explained
The FDR is based on a complex algorithm developed by FPL experts.
A set of formulas process key Opta data variables, along with each team's home and away form for the past six matches, to generate a rank for the perceived difficulty of each Gameweek opponent.
The FDR is designed to help FPL managers plan their transfer and team selection strategy. It is reviewed on a weekly basis and updated as the season progresses.

So what players should you buy? Looking first at Bournemouth, there are several good options.

Kluivert

If you don't have Kluivert in your team, it's a great time to being him in. As can be seen in the 'Price versus Total Points' graph on the graphs page, Kluivert consistently outperforms his low in-game price tag, amassing 140 total points for only £6.1M. He should be a priority purchase over the next three gameweeks.

Evanilson

Evanilson has recently returned from injury and is expected to be Bournemouth's starting striker for the foreseeable future. He is a good budget striker pick, at only £5.6M. Evanilson's return puts O.Dango's minutes into doubt, he may not see many starts in the future.

Semenyo

A solid option in midfield, although he may now see fewer minutes as injured players return to the Bournemouth squad.

Kerkes

Kerkes has been in good form recently, getting an assist in each of his last 2 gameweeks. With Bournemouth's run of relatively easy matches, he should notch up more points for clean sheets and assists over the next few weeks.

Arrizabalaga

The Bournemouth goalkeeper is on loan from Chelsea, having spent last season in Real Madrid. He is one of the best goalkeeper choices right now owing to Bournemouth's kind fixtures.

After that, Semenyo, Kerkes or Aït-Nouri are all good players to have in your squad. Neither goalkeeper is a priority buy, as the variance in goalkeeper points is generally far lower than the variance in outfield player points.

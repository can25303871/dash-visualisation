Fantasy Premier League: a brief overview

Fantasy Premier League (FPL) is a popular mobile and web-based game where a user starts with a Â£100M budget, and picks a squad of fifteen players from the Premier League. Different players cost different amounts depending on their past performances or real-world price. From these fifteen, the user picks eleven starting players and four substitutes, with constraints such as 1 goalkeeper and minimum 3 defenders. This starting eleven must contain a captain, who earns double points, and a vice-captain, who assumes the captaincy if the captain doesn't play. The substitutes on a users bench will come onto the team if one of their starting eleven doesn't play that week, meaning it is important to have a strong bench The user earns points based on the performance of their 11 selected players, depending on the positions of these players. For example, goalkeepers and defenders earn four points for a clean sheet, and forwards earn four points for scoring a goal. All players earn 3 points for an assist and 2 points for playing over 60 minutes. Players can also lose points, for yellow and red cards, own goals or missed penalties. A full breakdown of points is available here on the Premier League website.

Users can use one free transfer per week to buy and sell players, after the free transfer is used they are deducted points for subsequent transfers. These free transfers accumulate up to five if unused. This means that long term planning is an important part of the game, it is a bad idea to buy a player with one easy game then a run of tougher games.

Users also have access to what are called 'chips', such as bench boost or assistant manager. These have special functions in the game and each one can generally only be used once a season. For example, bench boost makes it so that all fifteen players in a user's squad will earn points, not just the starters. Assistant manager allows a user to buy a manager and earn points based on their team's performance, such as winning, drawing, scoring, or keeping a clean sheet.

Dataset Overview

The Fantasy Premier League dataset includes a multitude of different Premier League data, such as player statistics and team performance.

Key Features of the Dataset

  • Player Statistics: The dataset includes detailed information about players and managers, such as:
    • Goals scored
    • Assists
    • Minutes played
    • Yellow and red cards
    • Clean sheets (for defenders, goalkeepers, and midfielders)
    • Wins, draws, and losses for managers.
  • Team Performance: The dataset also includes team performance information, such as:
    • Total goals scored and conceded
    • Wins, draws, and losses
    • Home and away performance
    • Form over the last 5 matches

Dataset Structure

The dataset is structured into several files, focusing on different Premier League data:

  • Players: The dataset contains individual player statistics, including position, team, and performance information such as goals and assists.
  • Teams: Information such as total goals, wins, and form is provided.
  • Fixtures: Fixture info is included for each team, such as dates, venues, and results.

Relevance of the dataset

The Fantasy Premier League dataset is highly relevant for:

  • FPL users: Helps users make decisions about team selection, transfers, and captain choices.
  • Data Analysts Player and team performance can be easily analysed over time.
  • Other Premier League Enthusiasts: The dataset contains a wealth of information about players and teams.

Insights: Examples

Here are some examples of possible insights from the dataset:

  • Top Scorers: Players with the most goals or assists in a season can be easily identified.
  • Team Form: Analyse which teams are in the best form over the last 5 matches.
  • User Preferences: Determine the most popular players in the in-game teams of Fantasy Premier League users.

Data Source

The data is sourced from Fantasy Premier League GitHub. You can find more information there. The data is updated weekly, after all matches have finished.
